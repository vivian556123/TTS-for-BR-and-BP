Advancing Zero-Shot Text-to-Speech System
for Background Removal and Preservation
with Controllable Masked Speech Prediction


Abstract

Despite recent advancements in zero-shot Text-to-Speech technologies, current systems often struggle with low-quality prompts contaminated by environmental noise, reverberation, or interfering speakers, leading to degraded speech output. The appropriate handling of these acoustic conditions is context-dependent: while background noise may need to be removed for clarity, preserving the acoustic environment is sometimes crucial to maintaining the contextual integrity of the speech. To address this dual requirement, we propose a Controllable Masked Speech Prediction (CMSP) strategy coupled with a dual-speaker encoder, integrated into a flow-matching-based zero-shot TTS model. Experimental results demonstrate that our approach enables precise control over the removal or preservation of background across various acoustic conditions and exhibits strong generalization capabilities in unseen scenarios.
